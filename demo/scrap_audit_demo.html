SCRAP — Trustless Authorization & Settlement for On-Orbit Compute

A customer buys AI inference on another operator's satellite. SCRAP introduces no trust roots: it provides verifiable authorization, atomic payment-for-delivery, and a non-repudiable evidence trail — all checkable from public keys alone. It does not police what the executor actually did; that stays a governance matter.

1Authorization packet SAT-CAP capability token

Operator A signs exactly what is permitted: one capability, plus policy terms (the model that may run, a runtime ceiling, a data-sovereignty region). This is a signed statement of permission — capabilities can only narrow downstream, never widen. It authorizes; it does not enforce.


    

  


  
  

    
2Execution receipt signed delivery proof + attestation

    
The node returns a receipt binding the input it was given, the output it delivered, and the payment hash, under its signature. It also attests which model ran and in which region — a non-repudiable claim, not verified by SCRAP (highlighted below). SCRAP proves delivery and binds the claim to payment; it does not check whether the claim is true.

    

    

  


  
  

    
3Independent verification no secret keys required

    
Anyone — the customer, a counterparty, a governance body like Kenya's NEODC — re-checks the records later from public keys only. Every check below is decidable from signatures alone, with no external trust root and no oracle. They establish authenticity, scope, and atomic payment-for-delivery — never the truth of an off-chain claim.

    What SCRAP deliberately does NOT do
    
      
    SCRAP makes no claim about what physically happened off-chain — whether the node truly ran eo-segnet-v3, or whether the result stayed in Kenya. Verifying that would require a trust root SCRAP refuses to be (a TEE quote, key-custody, a staking/slashing oracle). Instead, the executor's attestation is non-repudiable: if it later proves false, the signature is the evidence for a governance or contractual remedy. Enforcement is a separate, optional layer owned by the data-owner and bodies like the National Earth Observation Data Council — SCRAP just supplies the incorruptible record they adjudicate with.
    
    
    
  
    

  
  
    
    
    4Atomic settlement timeout-default fair exchange
    
    
    Payment and delivery are bound by one secret. The payment HTLC is locked to payment_hash H; the executor can only claim it by revealing the preimage R (where SHA256(R)=H) — and revealing R is delivering the receipt. No trusted escrow: either the executor delivers and is paid, or the timeout refunds the customer. SCRAP spec §6.8, "Proof Model: Delivery Only."

    5Integration as an optional layer
    
    
    SCRAP is additive. It follows OGC-API / STAPI conformance classes — an operator advertises only what they run. An existing compute marketplace adopts it with one adapter: accepted job order → signed capability token. Settlement stays opt-in.
    
    
    
      
    /conf/coreREQUIRED landing · conformance · operator identity (pubkey)
    
      
    /conf/nodesREQUIRED compute-node catalog (maps to STAPI products)
    
      
    /conf/tokensREQUIRED POST /tokens — issues SAT-CAP (replaces marketplace order)
    
      
    /conf/receiptsOPTIONAL fetch / re-verify execution receipts
    
      
    /conf/lightningOPTIONAL payment binding & settlement (Dyson-operated coordination)
